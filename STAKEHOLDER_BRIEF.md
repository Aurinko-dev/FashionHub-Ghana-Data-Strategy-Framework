# 📋 Stakeholder Brief — FashionHub Ghana Data Strategy

**For:** Board Members, Investors, Non-Technical Stakeholders
**Prepared by:** Group 9 — OMIS 407, University of Ghana Business School
**Date:** 2025

---

## The Situation in One Paragraph

FashionHub Ghana is a promising e-commerce startup selling Ghanaian-designed fashion online. In its first month, the company generated GHS 450,000 in revenue — but posted a GHS 90,000 loss. The business is not failing because of weak demand. It is failing because **every major decision is made without data**. Orders arrive via WhatsApp. Customer records live in phone contacts. 30% of deliveries fail because addresses are incomplete. And 85% of customers never return — not because they disliked the product, but because no system exists to bring them back.

---

## What the Board Must Decide

| Decision | Options | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Fund data infrastructure? | Yes / No | **Yes — GHS 2,500/month; 62x ROI** |
| Hire tech partner? | Week 1 / Later | **Week 1 — gates everything else** |
| Migrate from WhatsApp? | Now / Wait | **Now — every week costs data** |

## What Happens If We Do Nothing

- Losses compound as the business acquires more customers it cannot retain
- Delivery failures continue to erode brand trust in a market where trust is scarce
- Competitors with even basic analytics infrastructure will outperform FashionHub on every dimension
- The business reaches a funding or cash flow ceiling with no data to show investors

---

## Board Recommendation

**Fund this strategy.** The GHS 2,500/month investment in technology is the highest-return capital allocation available to FashionHub. It addresses the root cause of the losses (not the symptoms), builds lasting competitive capability, and creates the data infrastructure needed to attract future investment.

The question is not whether to invest in data. The question is how much longer to operate without it.
